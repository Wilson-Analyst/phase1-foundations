User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) design have emerged as critical determinants
of competitive advantage in the rapidly evolving e-commerce landscape, directly influencing
user engagement, satisfaction, and platform adoption rates. Despite extensive recognition of
UI/UX importance, existing evaluation methodologies predominantly rely on descriptive statistical
approaches such as Mean Opinion Score (MOS) calculations, which provide valuable insights into
user perceptions but lack the inferential statistical rigor necessary to quantify relationships
between specific design elements and overall satisfaction outcomes. To address this methodological
 gap, the present study extends the baseline MOS framework by integrating Pearson correlation 
analysis and multiple linear regression to quantify the associations between three UI/UX design 
aspects (homepage design, navigation interface, and color theory) and overall user satisfaction,
enabling identification of the most influential predictors while controlling for confounding
effects. Analysis of data from four Indonesian e-commerce platforms (Shopee, Tokopedia, Blibli,
and Lazada) revealed that homepage design demonstrated the strongest and only statistically 
significant correlation with overall satisfaction (r = 0.9556, p = 0.0444), while the regression
model explained 100 percent of variance with navigation interface emerging as the most influential
predictor (β = 0.3362), thereby validating the original study's descriptive findings through 
inferential statistics and demonstrating that advanced statistical methods can enhance 
interpretability, establish predictive relationships, and provide actionable insights for UI/UX
optimization in e-commerce contexts.
